Memorandum: Defeating "No Damages for Delay" in Florida Public Projects
Construction Litigation Florida 2021

Comprehensive internal memo analyzing Florida's approach to "no damages for delay" clauses. Identifies five recognized exceptions under Florida law: (1) active interference, (2) bad faith, (3) delays beyond contract scope, (4) fraud or misrepresentation, and (5) abandonment of the contract. Notes that the Third DCA in Marriott Corp. v. Dasta Constr. remains the leading authority. Recommends always pleading active interference as the primary theory.

Brief: Opposition to MSJ — Oceanfront Towers Delay Claim (2019)
Motion Practice Miami-Dade 2019

Successfully defeated Owner's summary judgment motion relying on a "no damages for delay" clause. Key argument: Owner's refusal to process 14 RFIs for 30+ days each constituted active interference, falling squarely within the exception. Court denied MSJ in full. Judge Fernandez noted: "The Owner cannot create the delay and then hide behind the clause."

Partner Alert: New 4th DCA Ruling on Delay Damages — SunBridge Holdings (2023)
Case Alert 4th DCA 2023

Analysis of SunBridge Holdings v. Apex Builders (4th DCA 2023), which expanded the "active interference" exception. Court held that repeated design changes after contract execution constitute active interference even if each individual change was within the Owner's contractual right. Particularly relevant where cumulative impact exceeds 15% of original schedule.
